Research

Galaxy Evolution &
Machine Learning

My master's research focuses on photometric galaxy classification using machine learning — eliminating the need for expensive spectroscopic observations. I am developing SpecLess, a program designed to classify galaxies through machine learning based purely on photometric data.

My work combines observational astronomy with modern data-driven approaches, including SED modelling and astrostatistical methods. I contribute to the LSST Informatics & Statistics Science Collaboration (ISSC) at the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, supporting large-scale astronomical dataset analysis.

My bachelor's thesis examined the relationship between Starburst Galaxies and Active Galactic Nuclei, using photometric and spectral methods on large data samples.

"Scientific data gains meaning not only through observation but through reasoning and algorithms."
